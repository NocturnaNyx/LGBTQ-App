Gender Identities


Agender

A term used to describe one who identifies as not having a gender.


Androgynous

A term used to describe a person who possess characteristics of both the male and female genders.

Bigender

A term to describe a person who identifies with both the male and female genders.

Binary

A term used to describe the belief that there are only two genders, male and female.

Cisgender

A term used to describe people whose gender identity aligns with the sex they were assigned at birth.

Demigender

A term used to describe a person who identifies with certain elements of one gender, varying in intensity depending on the person. Common subcategories include demigirl and demiboy.

Female

A term to describe one of the two binary sexes.

FTM

A person who has undergone the medical transition from female to male.

Gender Fluid

A term referring to a person who does not identify with any fixed gender.

Gender Flux

A term used to describe a person whose gender identity fluctuates in intensity.

Intergender

A term used to describe a person whose gender identity is in the middle of the binary genders or contains elements of both the male and female gender identities.

Intersex

A term to describe those who are born with physical sex markers that are not clearly male or female.

Male

A term to describe one of the two binary sexes.

MTF

A person who has undergone the medical transition from male to female.

Neutrois

A person who identifies as having a neutral gender unassociated with the male and female genders.

Nonbinary

Also referred to as gender queer, an umbrella term used to describe any person who does not identify as male or female.

Pangender

A term used to describe a person who identifies as all genders.

Polygender

A term used to refer to those who experience multiple gender identities at once.

Third Gender

A term used to describe a person who does not identify as male or female.

Transgender

An often misconceived umbrella term that refers to people whose gender identities do not match the sex they were assigned at birth.

Two Spirit

An umbrella term used mainly by the Native American LGBTQ+ community to describe people who identify as nonbinary.


Sexual Orientations

Asexual

An umbrella term that describes people who do not experience sexual attraction to others.

Pansexual

A term used to describe a person who is sexually attracted to others of any gender identity.

Romantic Preferences

Polyamorous

A term used to describe a person who desires a relationship containing more than the traditionally accepted two members. Polyamory is often misconstrued as being unfaithful to one’s partner, however this is false as polyamorous relationships occur with the full consent of all involved parties.

Panromantic

A term used to describe a person who is romantically attracted to others of any gender identity.

Miscellaneous Terms

Gender Dysphoria

A term used to describe the psychological and emotional conflict that a nonbinary person feels in relation to differences between their physical sex and their gender identity. Gender dysphoria can be extremely distressing and cause lasting psychological damage to nonbinary and transgender people.

Gender Non-Conforming

A term used to describe those whose behaviours and appearance do not align with societal norms regarding to their gender identity.
